Cover letter

The full case.

Why the publisher-side perspective matters for this role.

"Most affiliate managers have worked with publishers. I've worked as one."

For four years across TechRadar Italia and Tom's Hardware, I was on the side of the table that decides which programs get promoted, which deals get featured, and which partnerships get prioritized when editorial space is limited. I know what makes a publisher say yes, and what makes them quietly deprioritize a partner regardless of payout. That perspective is difficult to replicate from the advertiser side, and it is directly relevant to this role.

I moved from Senior Editor to my current role at Blazemedia deliberately: I wanted to take the publisher-side understanding I had built and apply it operationally, from inside an affiliate content operation rather than from inside a newsroom. At Blazemedia, I lead a team of over ten people responsible for affiliate content across Amazon, eBay, and major tech retailers. My responsibilities include hiring and onboarding new team members, managing individual performance, and owning quality standards across the full output. The team generates over €40,000 in monthly revenue from promotional content alone, primarily across VPN, telecom, and subscription services. I work through Awin for partner tracking and link management, and I coordinate daily across editorial, commercial, and tech teams to connect content decisions to measurable revenue outcomes.

My title is Affiliate Content Coordinator, but the operational scope has been full team ownership, hiring accountability, and performance-driven decision-making since day one.

Before Blazemedia, as Senior Editor at TechRadar Italia and Tom's Hardware, I managed editorial partnerships with VPN providers, cloud services, and eLearning brands in one of the most competitive affiliate verticals in European tech media. I understand how publishers evaluate long-term relationships, what signals make them scale a partnership, and how trust gets built or lost in the first ninety days of a new deal.

My approach is data-driven by necessity. I track what converts, iterate on what does not, and make decisions quickly when the data is ambiguous. I have operated in fast-paced environments where the cost of waiting for certainty is higher than the cost of a wrong move that can be corrected.

I am fully remote-based and have worked in international environments throughout my career. My English is C1, operational, and has been my primary professional language for over a decade.

I have a clear view of what I would build in this role and how I would start. I am ready to discuss it.
